Players

Roger Federer

He has won 20 Grand Slam singles titles, the most in history for a male player, and has held the world No. 1 spot in the ATP rankings for a record total of 310 weeks.

Rafael Nadal

He has won a record 12 French Open titles, 4 US Open titles, 2 Wimbledon titles and 1 Australian Open title, and won at least one Grand Slam every year for 10 consecutive years.

Novak Djokovic

He has won 17 Grand Slam singles titles, the third-most in history for a male player, five ATP Finals titles, 34 ATP Tour Masters 1000 titles, and has held the No. 1 spot in the ATP rankings for 276 weeks.

Dominic Thiem

His career-high ATP ranking is world No. 4, which he first achieved on 6 November 2017. He has won 16 ATP singles titles and reached three Grand Slam finals.

Alexander Zverev

He is the second-youngest player ranked in the top 10. Zverev was the champion at the 2018 ATP Finals.

Stan Wawrinka

His career highlights include three Grand Slam titles at Australian Open, French Open and US Open, where he defeated the No. 1 player in the final on all three occasions.

Daniil Medvedev

He has won seven ATP singles titles, including two Masters 1000 titles. He reached six consecutive tournament finals, including one at the 2019 US Open and three at Masters 1000 events.

Stefanos Tsitsipas

He is the youngest player ranked in the top 10. Tsitsipas is the reigning champion at the ATP Finals, He has won four singles titles and reached nine finals on the ATP Tour.
